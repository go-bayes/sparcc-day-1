Welcome to SPARCC Day 1! 🌈

Building Foundations for Causal Thinking in the Study of Religion and Cooperation

Tuesday, 12 August 2025 | 09:00 – 17:00

Workshop Overview

This workshop introduces the fundamental concepts and practical skills for causal inference in observational data. We’ll explore how to move beyond correlation to understand causation in the study of religion and cooperation.

Schedule

Learning Objectives

By the end of Day 1, participants will be able to:

🎯 Core Skills

  1. Think causally about correlations in observational data
  2. Draw and interpret causal diagrams (DAGs)
  3. Identify confounders, colliders, and other sources of bias
  4. Apply the EESI workflow to structure research projects
  5. Select appropriate control variables for causal analyses
  6. Understand basic estimation approaches (g-computation, IPW, doubly robust methods)

What You’ll Need

TipPrerequisites
  • Basic familiarity with statistical concepts (regression, correlation)
  • Interest in causal questions about religion and cooperation
  • No prior causal inference experience required!
NoteMaterials to Bring
  • Laptop with R and RStudio installed (optional but helpful)
  • Your research questions about religion and cooperation
  • Enthusiasm for collaborative learning!

Workshop Philosophy

This workshop emphasizes learning by doing. We’ll work through practical examples, engage in group exercises, and apply concepts to real research questions in the study of religion and cooperation.

“Correlation does not imply causation, but it sure is a hint!”

## Learning Objectives

By the end of Day 1, participants will be able to:

::: {.card}
### 🎯 Core Skills

1. **Think causally** about correlations in observational data
2. **Draw and interpret** causal diagrams (DAGs)
3. **Identify** confounders, colliders, and other sources of bias
4. **Apply** the EESI workflow to structure research projects
5. **Select** appropriate control variables for causal analyses
